TL;DR

A 2019 queer hockey romance novel, 'Heated Rivalry' by Rachel Reid, has experienced an explosive resurgence in popularity, rocketing to the top of bestseller lists years after its initial release. This revival is not the result of a traditional marketing campaign, but rather an organic, fan-driven phenomenon on TikTok. A dedicated community of readers has created and circulated thousands of 'fan edits'—short, evocative video montages set to music—that capture the intense, decade-long secret romance between the book's rival protagonists. These edits, often steamier and more emotionally charged than a movie trailer, have created a viral feedback loop, introducing the novel to a massive new audience and demonstrating the profound power of fan culture in the digital age.

Key Data Points

  • Rachel Reid's 2019 novel 'Heated Rivalry' has become a viral sensation on TikTok's 'BookTok' community, leading to a significant surge in sales and mainstream attention.
  • The book centers on the secret, long-term 'enemies-to-lovers' romance between two fictional rival NHL players, Ilya Rozanov and Shane Hollander.
  • The trend is primarily driven by fan-created 'edits,' which are short video montages using music and 'fan-casted' actors to convey the novel's romantic and emotional intensity.
  • This phenomenon highlights the ability of online fan communities to resurrect backlist titles, effectively creating powerful, user-generated marketing campaigns that can outperform traditional efforts.
  • The widespread popularity of 'Heated Rivalry' underscores a growing mainstream appetite for queer romance narratives, particularly those set within traditionally hyper-masculine spaces like professional sports.

Deep Dive

First published in 2019, 'Heated Rivalry' is the second installment in Rachel Reid's 'Game Changers' series, but it can be read as a standalone. The novel chronicles the complex relationship between two elite hockey players: the stoic, disciplined Canadian captain Shane Hollander and his flamboyant, outspoken Russian rival, Ilya Rozanov. Their public rivalry masks a secret, intensely passionate connection that unfolds over nearly a decade through clandestine meetings in hotel rooms around the world. The narrative leverages the popular 'enemies-to-lovers' trope, but its depth comes from the slow-burn development of their bond, exploring themes of hidden identity, vulnerability, and the conflict between public personas and private selves. While the book was well-regarded within the romance community upon its release, its recent ascent to cultural touchstone status is a distinctly digital-era story.

The catalyst for this resurgence is the 'fan edit.' On platforms like TikTok, fans have taken the emotional core of Reid's novel and translated it into a visual, shareable format. These edits are sophisticated pieces of micro-storytelling. Creators 'fan-cast' actors—often selecting figures like Swedish actor Bill Skarsgård for Ilya Rozanov—and use clips from their filmographies, pairing them with evocative music. Songs like Hozier’s 'Work Song' have become anthems for the characters' relationship, with lyrics and melody perfectly synched to visuals that convey longing, rivalry, and passion. This creative output does more than just celebrate the book; it functions as a highly effective, emotionally resonant advertisement. The sheer volume of these edits, garnering millions of views, has created an inescapable presence on the 'For You' pages of countless potential readers, compelling them to discover the source of this shared cultural obsession. The fan edits are not just summaries; they are interpretations that amplify the novel's central romantic conflict, making it irresistible to a new generation of readers.

Implications

For the publishing industry, the 'Heated Rivalry' phenomenon is a powerful case study in the shifting dynamics of book marketing. It proves that a book's commercial lifecycle is no longer finite and that backlist titles possess immense, untapped potential. The most potent marketing tool is no longer a publisher-led campaign but an authentic, passionate endorsement from a fan community. Platforms like TikTok have democratized literary tastemaking, placing the power to create a bestseller directly in the hands of readers. This suggests a future where publishers may focus less on initial launch hype and more on fostering and engaging with the fan communities that can give a book a second, third, or even fourth life years down the line. The return on investment from a single viral video created by a fan can exceed that of a costly, traditional advertising spend.

Culturally, the success of 'Heated Rivalry' signals a significant moment for queer romance and fan culture. The story's setting within the hyper-masculine world of professional hockey provides a compelling backdrop for a tender and passionate M/M romance, challenging traditional genre boundaries and finding a broad, enthusiastic audience. This mainstream acceptance demonstrates a clear market demand for such narratives. Furthermore, the trend validates the creative labor of fan communities. For decades, fanfiction, fan art, and fan videos were often dismissed as niche or derivative hobbies. Now, these creative expressions are recognized as powerful cultural drivers, capable of shaping popular taste and directly impacting commercial success. The line between content consumption and content creation is increasingly blurred, with fans acting as co-creators of a book's cultural meaning and legacy.

Contrarian View

While the viral success driven by fan edits is a clear sales boon, it may also create a distorted perception of the novel for incoming readers. The edits, by nature, focus on high-impact moments of angst and passion, potentially setting an expectation for a story that is purely composed of dramatic, cinematic scenes. New readers drawn in by the steamy, fast-paced TikTok clips might be unprepared for the novel's slower, more nuanced character development and the decade-long timeline, possibly leading to a disconnect between the viral phenomenon and the actual reading experience. This intense, singular focus on the 'steamiest' aspects could overshadow the book's deeper emotional narrative.

FAQ

  • What is 'Heated Rivalry' about? 'Heated Rivalry' is a 2019 M/M romance novel by Rachel Reid. It follows the secret, decade-long relationship between two rival NHL superstars, the reserved Shane Hollander and the charismatic Ilya Rozanov, exploring an 'enemies-to-lovers' dynamic.
  • Why is a book from 2019 suddenly so popular? Its popularity surged dramatically in 2023 and 2024 after it went viral on TikTok's 'BookTok' community. Fans began creating and sharing thousands of video 'edits' that showcased the book's romantic tension, introducing it to a massive new audience.
  • What are the 'fan edits' everyone is talking about? Fan edits are short, fan-made videos, typically for TikTok, that function like mini-trailers. They combine images, video clips (often of actors 'fan-cast' in the roles), and music to create a powerful, emotional snapshot of the book's story and characters.
  • Is 'Heated Rivalry' a standalone novel? It is the second book in the 'Game Changers' series, but the story is self-contained and widely read as a standalone novel. The fifth book in the series, 'The Long Game,' continues Ilya and Shane's story.
