Q3 streaming trends: Why Spotify’s growth in the Global South matters for the music industry - MIDiA Research

TL;DR

The latest Q3 2023 music streaming data signals a pivotal geographic shift in the industry's growth engine. While global subscriber numbers continue to climb, reaching over 713 million, the narrative is no longer solely about established Western markets. Instead, the spotlight is firmly on the 'Global South'—the emerging markets across Latin America, Asia, and Africa. Spotify, the undisputed market leader, exemplifies this trend, with the vast majority of its new user growth now originating from these regions. This migration from high-revenue, saturated markets to high-volume, lower-revenue territories marks a new chapter for streaming, forcing platforms and rightsholders to rethink their strategies for monetization, content, and global expansion.

Key Data Points

  • Spotify's user growth is now overwhelmingly driven by emerging markets, with 88% of its new monthly active users in 2023 coming from its 'Rest of World' category.
  • While Spotify maintains its top market share at 31.7%, its lead is being challenged by fast-growing competitors like YouTube Music, which grew its subscriber base by over 50% year-on-year.
  • The music streaming industry is transitioning from a 'growth-at-all-costs' phase to a focus on profitability, creating a strategic tension with the acquisition of lower-revenue users from the Global South.
  • Monetizing the Global South requires new, localized strategies that balance massive user scale with a lower Average Revenue Per User (ARPU).
  • Recent price hikes in mature markets like North America and Europe are a direct strategy by services like Spotify to offset the financial impact of this geographic growth shift and improve overall profitability.
  • The total global music subscriber count reached 713.4 million at the end of Q3 2023, an increase of 90.1 million from the previous year.

Deep Dive

The numbers from Q3 2023 paint a clear picture of a market in transition. According to MIDiA Research, the global music subscriber base swelled to 713.4 million, with Spotify leading the pack. The Swedish streaming giant reached 226 million premium subscribers and a total of 574 million monthly active users (MAUs). However, the most telling statistic is the origin of this growth. Of the 26 million MAUs Spotify added in the first three quarters of 2023, a staggering 23 million—or 88%—came from its 'Rest of World' segment, which encompasses Latin America, Asia, Africa, and the Middle East. This is a dramatic acceleration from 2020, when this segment accounted for only 30% of MAU growth. This data confirms that the well of new users in North America and Europe is running dry, and the future of streaming volume lies squarely in emerging economies.

While Spotify's dominance in absolute numbers is secure for now, its market share has stabilized at 31.7%, down from a peak of 33.2% in 2020. This indicates a highly competitive landscape where rivals are carving out significant niches. Tencent Music, with its 16.6% share, remains a powerhouse largely due to its dominance in China. Meanwhile, YouTube Music has emerged as the sector's fastest-growing player, increasing its subscriber count by over 50% year-on-year to capture 9.7% of the market. This competitive pressure, combined with the geographic shift, means Spotify cannot afford to be complacent. Success in the 'Global South' is not a monolithic challenge; it requires a deep, nuanced understanding of dozens of unique local markets, each with its own cultural tastes, economic conditions, and licensing complexities. The era of a one-size-fits-all global strategy is officially over.

Implications

The most immediate implication of this trend is the monetization conundrum. The Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) in markets like India or Brazil is significantly lower than in the United States or Germany. As the user base becomes increasingly weighted towards these lower-ARPU regions, it puts downward pressure on a company's overall revenue metrics and, consequently, the royalty pool available for artists and rightsholders. This financial reality is the primary driver behind Spotify's recent and widespread price increases in its mature, established markets. By extracting more value from its loyal, high-ARPU user base, Spotify can financially sustain its expansion into less profitable, high-growth territories. This creates a delicate balancing act: growing the global user count without diluting the overall value of a subscription, a challenge that will define the industry's financial health for years to come.

Beyond the balance sheet, this shift represents a profound cultural and strategic reorientation for the entire music industry. For decades, the path to global stardom ran through Western media markets. Now, the industry's center of gravity is decentralizing. The future of music discovery, artist development, and revenue growth is becoming increasingly tied to local ecosystems. Streaming platforms and record labels must invest heavily in local artist and repertoire (A&R), curate hyper-local playlists, and develop marketing campaigns that resonate with diverse cultural contexts. This means success will be measured not just by breaking an American artist in Europe, but by turning a Nigerian Afrobeats star into a global phenomenon or elevating a K-Pop group beyond its home region. The industry is moving from a model of exporting Western culture to one of cultivating a truly global, multi-polar network of musical exchange.

Contrarian View

While the scale of user growth in the Global South is impressive, it could be a 'vanity metric' if it cannot be effectively monetized. The industry might be trading a smaller number of high-value, profitable subscribers in mature markets for a vast number of low-margin users. This risks creating a long-term model where the platform's user count soars but its ability to generate meaningful profit and increase royalty payouts stagnates, potentially devaluing music and straining the entire streaming ecosystem.

FAQ

  • Who is the current market leader in music streaming? As of Q3 2023, Spotify remains the global market leader with a 31.7% share of music subscribers, totaling 226 million premium users and 574 million monthly active users.
  • Why is growth in the 'Global South' so important for streaming services? The 'Global South' represents the largest remaining pool of potential new subscribers. With markets in North America and Europe nearing saturation, these emerging regions are critical for the future user growth of services like Spotify and the music industry as a whole.
  • How are streaming services balancing low-revenue user growth with profitability? They are employing a dual strategy: aggressively acquiring users in emerging markets with competitive, lower-priced plans while simultaneously increasing subscription prices in mature, high-revenue markets to boost overall average revenue and fund global expansion.
