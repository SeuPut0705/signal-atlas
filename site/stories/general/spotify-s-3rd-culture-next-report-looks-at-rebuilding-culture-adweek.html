GeneralSpotify’s 3rd Culture Next Report Looks at Rebuilding Culture - ADWEEKSpotify's 2022 Culture Next report reveals how Gen Z and Millennials are using audio, creators, and podcasts to rebuild culture, community, and identity.

TL;DR

In a world defined by constant flux and information overload, Spotify's third annual Culture Next report reveals a powerful counter-movement led by Gen Z and Millennials. These generations are not passively consuming culture; they are actively rebuilding it from the ground up, using audio as their primary tool. The report, a comprehensive global study, unpacks how younger audiences are leveraging creators, podcasts, and music to navigate a 'post-everything' landscape, seeking authenticity, community, and nuance in ways that challenge traditional media and marketing paradigms. This briefing explores the core findings, from the rise of creators as trusted cultural guides to the role of audio as a sanctuary for mental wellness and self-discovery.

Key Data Points

  • Gen Z and Millennials are actively 'rebuilding' culture, using audio to find stability and connection in a chaotic world.
  • Creators have evolved from entertainers to essential guides, with 55% of Gen Z stating they trust creators more than they trust their friends online.
  • Podcasts are a critical medium for this generation, providing a space for nuanced conversations, mental health support, and deep community building.
  • Authenticity is the new currency; these generations are rejecting polished corporate messaging in favor of genuine, vulnerable, and relatable content.
  • For brands, the key to engagement is to shift from an advertiser's mindset to a creator's, focusing on adding value and fostering community rather than interruption.

Deep Dive

The central theme of the 2022 Culture Next report is the profound shift in the relationship between young audiences and content creators. The report posits that in an era of overwhelming choice and societal uncertainty, creators have become the new compass. For Gen Z, they are more than just influencers; they are trusted sources for navigating everything from niche hobbies to complex social issues. According to Spotify's findings, a significant majority of Gen Z (70%) feel they can connect with creators on a deeper level than with traditional celebrities because creators offer a more authentic and unfiltered perspective. This trust is foundational. It's built on a perceived shared experience and vulnerability, which makes creator recommendations and viewpoints highly influential. This dynamic has given rise to powerful creator-fan ecosystems where community is co-created. Fans are no longer passive recipients of content but active participants who contribute to the culture surrounding a creator, fostering a sense of belonging that mainstream media often fails to provide.

Parallel to the rise of the creator economy is the deepening role of audio, particularly podcasts, as a sanctuary for mental health and nuanced discourse. The report highlights that the intimacy of audio provides a unique space for listeners to unplug from visual overstimulation and engage with topics on a more personal level. Nearly two-thirds of Millennials and Gen Z agree that audio allows them to explore different sides of their personality, and a similar number believe podcast hosts sometimes feel like their friends. This parasocial relationship is a powerful vehicle for discussing sensitive topics like mental wellness, identity, and personal growth. Unlike the often performative nature of visual social media, podcasts facilitate longer, more thoughtful conversations that embrace complexity and ambiguity. This makes them an invaluable resource for generations actively seeking to understand themselves and the world around them, turning to audio for solace, education, and a genuine sense of connection that feels increasingly rare elsewhere.

Implications

For marketers and brands, the implications of these trends are transformative. The old playbook of interruptive advertising and polished, aspirational messaging is rapidly losing its effectiveness with younger demographics. The Culture Next report serves as a clear directive: to connect with Gen Z and Millennials, brands must adopt a creator's mindset. This means prioritizing authenticity over perfection, fostering genuine community engagement over chasing fleeting viral moments, and adding tangible value to consumers' lives. Successful strategies will involve deep, long-term partnerships with creators whose values align with the brand's, allowing them the creative freedom to communicate in their own voice. Furthermore, brands should consider creating their own audio content, not as a direct sales tool, but as a platform for building a community around shared interests and values. The goal is to become a welcome participant in the culture, not an intrusive advertiser trying to break into it.

On a broader societal level, this shift signifies a continued decentralization of cultural influence. Power is moving away from traditional gatekeepers like major media corporations and institutions and toward a distributed network of individual creators and their niche communities. This democratization of culture means that trends can emerge from anywhere and spread rapidly through these highly engaged networks. It also means that culture itself is becoming more fragmented and personalized. While this allows for greater diversity of expression and a stronger sense of belonging within specific subcultures, it also presents challenges in creating broad social cohesion. The reliance on creators for guidance and information underscores a growing trust deficit with established institutions, a trend that has far-reaching consequences for media, politics, and public discourse. The 'rebuilding' of culture is not just about new media formats; it's about a fundamental realignment of who we listen to and why.

Contrarian View

While the report champions the authenticity of the creator economy, a contrarian perspective would argue that this 'authenticity' is often a highly curated and monetized performance. The pressure on creators to maintain a relatable persona can lead to burnout and a new, more insidious form of advertising where the line between genuine recommendation and paid sponsorship is intentionally blurred. As brands become more adept at mimicking creator-led strategies, consumers, particularly savvy Gen Z, may become increasingly cynical, leading to a new wave of 'authenticity fatigue' where all brand-related content is viewed with suspicion, regardless of its format or sincerity.

FAQ

  • What is the main takeaway from Spotify's 2022 Culture Next report? The main takeaway is that Gen Z and Millennials are actively rebuilding culture in response to a chaotic world, using audio and creators as their primary tools for finding authenticity, community, and guidance.
  • Why are podcasts so important to Gen Z and Millennials according to the report? Podcasts offer an intimate, screen-free medium for deep dives into niche topics, nuanced conversations, and mental health discussions. Listeners form strong parasocial relationships with hosts, finding a sense of friendship and community.
  • How does the report suggest brands should adapt their marketing strategies? The report advises brands to move away from traditional advertising and adopt a 'creator mindset.' This involves prioritizing authenticity, building long-term communities, adding value, and partnering with creators in a way that respects their voice and audience.
  • What does the report mean by a 'post-everything' world? This refers to the current cultural landscape where young generations are navigating the fallout from a pandemic, economic instability, social upheaval, and information overload, leading them to seek new, more reliable sources of stability and truth.
